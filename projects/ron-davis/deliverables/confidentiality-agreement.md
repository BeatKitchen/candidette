# Mutual Confidentiality Agreement

**Between**: Candidette Campaigns ("Consultant") and Friends of Ron Davis ("Campaign")

**Effective Date**: March 10, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This Mutual Confidentiality Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Elizabeth Rosenberg, doing business as Candidette Campaigns ("Consultant"), and Friends of Ron Davis, a political campaign committee ("Campaign"), in connection with a potential or ongoing campaign management engagement for Ron Davis, candidate for Washington State House of Representatives, 46th Legislative District.

The parties wish to share certain confidential information with each other for the purpose of exploring and executing this engagement, and agree to protect that information as set forth below.

---

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either party to the other, whether orally, in writing, or by any other means, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to:

**From the Campaign to the Consultant:**
- Candidate background, personal history, and sensitive biographical details
- Donor names, contact information, and giving history
- Internal polling, opposition research, and vulnerability assessments
- Campaign strategy, messaging, and targeting plans
- Financial information, budget, and expenditure plans
- Volunteer lists and field data
- Internal communications and deliberations

**From the Consultant to the Campaign:**
- Proprietary methods, systems, and approaches
- Client lists and prior campaign experience (to the extent not public)
- Vendor relationships and pricing
- Internal tools, templates, and materials
- Business and financial terms

---

## 3. Obligations

Each party agrees to:

1. Hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict confidence
2. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the disclosing party's prior written consent
3. Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of this engagement
4. Limit access to Confidential Information to individuals who need to know it for purposes of this engagement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement
5. Notify the other party promptly upon becoming aware of any unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information

---

## 4. Exceptions

These obligations do not apply to information that:

- Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement
- Was rightfully known to the receiving party before disclosure
- Is independently developed by the receiving party without use of the Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order — provided the receiving party gives the disclosing party prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order

---

## 5. Campaign Finance and Public Disclosure

The parties acknowledge that certain information — including campaign contributions, expenditures, and vendor contracts — may be subject to mandatory public disclosure under Washington State campaign finance law administered by the Public Disclosure Commission (PDC). Disclosure required by law does not constitute a breach of this Agreement.

---

## 6. Term

This Agreement takes effect on the Effective Date above and remains in effect for the duration of the engagement plus **two (2) years** following its conclusion, unless terminated earlier by mutual written agreement.

---

## 7. Return or Destruction of Information

Upon request or upon conclusion of the engagement, each party will promptly return or destroy the other party's Confidential Information, including all copies, notes, and summaries, except as required to be retained by law.

---

## 8. No License

Nothing in this Agreement grants either party any rights in the other party's Confidential Information except as expressly set forth herein.

---

## 9. Remedies

Each party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate, and that the non-breaching party is entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction, in addition to any other remedies available at law.

---

## 10. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Washington, without regard to conflict of law principles.

---

## 11. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding confidentiality and supersedes all prior discussions on the subject. It may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.
